Selling Options

Selling you casks

At Irish Whiskey Assets we will offer to buy your casks back from you after five years.

This option is what makes our business model truly unique. Our sister company, McCarthy’s Irish Whiskey, has a retail brand and so requires a constant supply of matured spirit.

Cask owners can sell their whiskey to McCarthy’s after it has matured for a least five years. They will buy your casks back at an agreed price, ensuring a profit for you the investor. While this is a straightforward and reliable option, cask owners are in no way obliged to take this option.

McCarthy’s Irish Whiskey is a premium, double-casked whiskey, which will be on sale in 11 countries from July 2019.

Other Options to Sell Your Casks

Once you decide to sell your casks, there are several options available to you. At Irish Whiskey Assets, we don’t place any restrictions on how you choose to sell. We will assist and guide you in all potential avenues of sale. Some of the options open to you could include:

Sell to another distillery

Industry projections show that Irish whiskey will be in short supply in the coming years, inevitably driving up prices. As production capacity is also limited and demand is continually growing, it’s reasonable to expect that casks will be highly sought after. Distilleries across the globe acquire whiskey this way, especially where their capacity is limited. It has become commonplace in Japan, for example. This is why your initial purchase amount is paramount, and why at Irish Whiskey Assets, we sell casks at such competitive prices. This allows enough margin for our cask owners to sell on within the trade.

Create own label or white label

This option could suit investors with a substantial number of casks, at least 10 or more. Owners could offer a white label/own label solution to chains of hotels, restaurants, off-licenses, retail outlets or pubs. Particularly where the end client specifies a unique finish, for example maturing the whiskey in different casks to create a distinctive taste. These casks might include wine, sherry, champagne or perhaps even stout. This options lends itself to a client who is seeking their own high-end, one of a kind premium whiskey. It could also be offered to clubs and other exclusive organisations.

Sell to another investor

If your casks have matured for 3 or more years, another investor may wish to purchase the set and allow them to mature for even longer. As the market is relatively new in Ireland, we believe this has the potential for great growth and will become a popular option in the future. Early investors can benefit hugely in this scenario, so it pays to act fast.

Auction your casks

In the last several years, a selection of specialist whiskey auction sites have emerged as a result of the renaissance in Irish whiskey. Some whiskeys have even reached record prices at auction, as there is a growing global demand for rare Irish whiskey. This option will be suited to those in possession of more mature casks.
