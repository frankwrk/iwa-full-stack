The order process

Getting Started

In fact, since 1990, Irish whiskey has been the fasted growing spirit in the world. The sector is undergoing a renaissance and export sales are forecast to double between 2015 and 2020 and double again by 2030. Irish whiskey exports are up 220% since 2007. Industry experts predict that Irish whiskey will struggle to keep pace with demand over the coming years, potentially leading to increased prices.

The combination of these factors makes a compelling case for investing in Irish whiskey. However, until now, investing in Irish whiskey was outside the scope of the average investor. This is where Irish Whiskey Assets is a game changer.

Step 1

Simply go online, select the cask package you wish to acquire, complete the short application form and upload your ID.

Step 2

Once we receive your cask order, we will then provide you with our secure bank transfer details.

Step 3

Once we receive your bank transfer, we issue a receipt and place your bespoke cask order with our award-winning partner distillery.

Step 4

Our partner distillery will schedule the distilling of your premium single malt spirit. This can take between 2 and 6 weeks, depending on production schedules.

Step 5

Once your single malt spirit is ready, we are notified by the distillery. They provide us with all the technical information supporting the production of your spirit. We set up an online account for you with full details of this information.

Step 6

Your single malt spirit is decanted into your casks on the distillery premises itself. Each cask is barcoded and registered in your name. It’s then safely and securely stored and insured in the distillery warehouse, under Bond.

Step 7

Your Certificate of Ownership and Certificate of Provenance are drafted, signed and sealed by Irish Whiskey Assets Ltd, and the distillery respectively. These certificates are then presented to you.

Step 8

Your casks remain protected at the distillery as they continue to age. After three years and one day, they earn the prestigious title of Irish Whiskey. Each of your casks is an appreciating asset containing a commodity in short supply and high demand.

Step 9

After five years of aging, your whiskey is ready for market should you so wish. Alternatively, you may also choose to continue to age your casks with the goal of achieving higher returns.
